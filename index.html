Digital Forensic is application of computer science involving securing the electronic media for the purpose of collection, examination, and presevation of data, recreation of events involving media to create a timeline, showing electronic media as a means or medium of happened activity in question and, creation of report that wil be held valid in court.

The application of science to the identification, collection, examination, and analysis, of data while preserving the integrity of the information and maintaining a strict chain of custody for the data.

source: NIST SP 800-86under Digital Forensics

the application of computer science and investigative procedures involving the examination of digital evidence - following proper search authority, chain of custody, validation with mathematics, use of validated tools, repeatability, reporting, and possibly expert testimony.

source: CNSSI 4009-2015 from DoDD 5505.13E

About company

Jad Saliba was working as a front-line police officer in Canada when he was diagnosed with cancer. After receiving treatment, Saliba was re-assigned to a digital forensic unit and quickly learned that the tools police agencies had at their disposal were insufficient to investigate cyber-enabled crimes. In short order, police agencies around the world sought out Saliba’s software to modernize their digital investigations. Saliba recognized an opportunity to do more for the pursuit of justice globally if he devoted himself to software development. In 2011, he left policing to launch Magnet Forensics with Adam Belsher.
